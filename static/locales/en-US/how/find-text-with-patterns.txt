Find and check text with patterns

A pattern describes the shape of some text, so you can check whether text fits a shape, or search for every place it appears. Patterns go inside \⣿ ⣿\ and read left to right, one piece at a time. \_\ is a letter, \#\ is a digit, \␣\ is a space, and quoted text like \"-"\ matches exactly.

To check whether a whole piece of text fits a pattern, use \≈\, which gives you a \?\:

\
'555-1234' ≈ ⣿3 # "-" 4 #⣿
\

That asks “three digits, a dash, then four digits?” — and the answer is \⊤\.

Counts go before the thing they repeat: \3 #\ means three digits. You can also say \>0 #\ (one or more), \≥0 #\ (zero or more), \≤1 #\ (optional), or a range like \2–4 #\.

To find every place a pattern appears, use \⌕\, which gives you a list of @Structure results. Name a piece with \name:( … )\ to capture it:

\
'born 2026-06-15' ⌕ ⣿y:(4 #) "-" m:(2 #) "-" d:(2 #)⣿
\

Each result tells you the matched \text\, where it \start\s and \end\s, and the captured \groups\ — here \y\, \m\, and \d\. Because patterns match by grapheme, they work the same in every language and script.
