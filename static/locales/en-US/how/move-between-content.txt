Move between content with arrow keys

Sometimes you want the audience to step through content one piece at a time, like a slide show, going forward /and/ back with the arrow keys.

Every version of this pattern is made of the same three parts:

• A @List of the content you want to show
• A @Key stream, which changes every time a key is pressed
• A @Reaction that remembers which piece of content you're on now

Here's the smallest version of it. Click the stage first so it's listening, then press the left and right arrow keys:

\
slides: ['first' 'second' 'third']

key: Key()

index•#: 1 … ∆ key …
	(key = 'ArrowRight') ? ((index < slides.length()) ? index + 1 index)
		(key = 'ArrowLeft') ? ((index > 1) ? index - 1 index)
			index

Phrase(slides[index])
\

The @Reaction is the heart of it. It starts at \1\, and every time the @Key changes, it decides what the new number should be.
If the right arrow was pressed, it adds one; if the left arrow was pressed, it subtracts one; otherwise it stays the same.

The extra \?\ checks are what keep the number from going past the ends of the list.
Without them you could walk off the end and there would be no slide to show.
If you'd rather wrap around from the last slide back to the first, you can do that instead.

¶Note: the arrow keys are named \'ArrowLeft'\, \'ArrowRight'\, \'ArrowUp'\, and \'ArrowDown'\.
You can also just write \'Left'\ and \'Right'\, and key names work in your own language too.¶

Notice that \index\ is only a number, so this works for /any/ kind of content, not just text.
Swap the @List for a list of @Group or whole scenes and the rest of the pattern doesn't change at all:

\
slides: [
	Phrase('a story in three parts' size: 3m)
	Group(Stack() [Phrase('🐈') Phrase('a cat appears')])
	Phrase('the end 🎉')
]

key: Key()

index•#: 1 … ∆ key …
	(key = 'ArrowRight') ? ((index < slides.length()) ? index + 1 index)
		(key = 'ArrowLeft') ? ((index > 1) ? index - 1 index)
			index

slides[index]
\
