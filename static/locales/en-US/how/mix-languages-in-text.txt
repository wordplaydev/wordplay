Greet a multilingual audience

A @Phrase shows the right translation to each viewer based on the languages they've chosen. If your audience speaks Spanish, you can speak Spanish to them; if they speak both Spanish and English — Spanglish! — you can mix.

Give a @TextLiteral several translations, including a multilingual one, and @wordplay picks the most fitting one for each viewer:

\
Phrase("hola"/es"hello"/en"hola gentleman"/es_en)
\

A viewer whose preferred language is just Spanish sees /hola/. A viewer who prefers just English sees /hello/. A viewer who prefers both Spanish and English sees /hola gentleman/ — the Spanglish version.

Wordplay favors the most specific match, so a monolingual translation always wins over a multilingual one when both could apply.
