Make your own character template

Sometimes you want lots of characters that look almost the same, but with little differences.
Instead of writing the same @Phrase over and over, you can make your own template with a @Structure.

A @Structure is like a recipe with blanks to fill in.
Here's a balloon template that takes a color and a label, and knows how to show itself:

\
•Balloon(color•Color label•'') (
    ƒ show() Phrase(`🎈 \label\` color: color size: 2m)
)

Group(Row() [
    Balloon(Color(80% 50 240°) 'Sky').show()
    Balloon(Color(60% 80 0°) 'Rose').show()
    Balloon(Color(90% 80 90°) 'Sunny').show()
])
\

The \•Balloon(...)\ part defines the template, and the \ƒ show()\ part says /how to draw one/.
Then we make three balloons with different colors and labels, and ask each one to \show\ itself.

Templates are great when you want to keep your code tidy and your characters consistent.
